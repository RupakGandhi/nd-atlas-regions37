How to Find the Right Resource

This directory includes behavioral health resources for children, youth, and families in Region 7 (Southeast North Dakota) and statewide providers. Use the filters on the left to narrow your search by service type, setting, or age group.

Tips for Navigating This Directory

  • Use the Care Pathway tabs above to filter by Clinical & Treatment, Prevention & Youth, or Community & Support services
  • Search by organization name, city, or service type in the search bar
  • Click any resource card to see full details including contact information and payment options
  • Use the Save button to bookmark resources and export a personalized list
  • One organization may appear multiple times if it provides more than one service type
  • Statewide resources serve families across all regions of North Dakota

Region 7 Continuum of Care

The continuum of care represents the full range of services available to children, youth, and families — from early prevention and wellness through intensive inpatient treatment. Services are organized from least to most intensive. Click any level to filter resources.

Prevention & Early Intervention

Social-emotional learning, early childhood supports, and community wellness programs that build resilience before problems arise.

Service Coordination

Telephonic or in-person care coordination connecting individuals and families with appropriate services, including FOCUS and Wraparound.

Outpatient & Integrated Care

Individual, group, and family therapy; medication management; and integrated behavioral health in primary care settings.

Intensive Home & Community

In-home services, intensive outpatient, partial hospitalization, MST, FFT, and wraparound care coordination for complex needs.

Comprehensive Crisis Care

988 crisis line, mobile crisis teams, crisis stabilization units, and emergency shelter services for immediate intervention.

Inpatient Care

Acute inpatient psychiatric treatment for individuals requiring 24-hour clinical supervision and stabilization.

Residential Treatment

PRTF, QRTP, and therapeutic group homes for youth requiring structured, clinically intensive residential care.

Peer-Led Services

Family and youth-led services including parent peer support, mentoring, training, and advocacy across the continuum.

Crosscutting Services: ND Federation of Families for Children's Mental Health, Family Voices of ND, Family Services Network (Parent Peer Support), and Special Education / School-based behavioral health services support families across all levels of the continuum.

Service Type Definitions

Understanding what each service type means can help you identify the right level of care for a child, youth, or family member.

About This Directory

This resource directory was developed by the North Dakota Department of Health and Human Services, Behavioral Health Division, in partnership with the Region 7 System of Care Grant steering committee.

The directory is designed to make behavioral health services accessible to professionals, families, and community members in the Southeast region of North Dakota, covering Cass, Ransom, Richland, Sargent, Dickey, and LaMoure counties.

Region 7 serves the Fargo-Moorhead metro area and surrounding rural Southeast ND counties. Statewide resources and tribal resources are included throughout this directory.
