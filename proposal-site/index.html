Background
Why This Platform Exists

Your team has done the hard work of mapping behavioral health resources across Regions 3 and 7. The challenge is making that data accessible — not just to providers, but to families, care coordinators, and community members who need it most.

The Problem with Spreadsheets

Your current asset mapping spreadsheets contain 386 records across two regions. They are comprehensive, accurate, and completely inaccessible to the families who need them. Spreadsheets cannot be searched by need, filtered by age group, or translated into Spanish or French for refugee and immigrant families.

What the Atlas Solves

  • Families can search by city, service type, age group, or need in seconds
  • Providers can filter by continuum level, setting, or specialized population
  • Care coordinators can save and export personalized resource lists
  • Spanish and French speakers can navigate the full directory in their language
  • An admin dashboard allows your team to update records without developer help

What Your Team Emphasized

  • The platform should educate users on the continuum of care, not just list resources
  • Service definitions need to be accessible to non-clinical community members
  • The Service Array visual should inform the platform structure and navigation
  • Rural and mobile-only users are a primary audience
  • Ownership, hosting, and long-term stewardship are key decision factors
  • CMHI System of Care grant funding is the likely payment vehicle

What We Have Already Built

  • Both prototypes are live and functional right now with your actual data
  • Region 3: 177 resources — teal theme, tribal service filters, Rolette/Towner/Cavalier counties
  • Region 7: 209 resources — blue theme, Bismarck/Fargo metro and surrounding counties
  • Full Spanish and French translations of the entire UI
  • Education panel with continuum of care, service definitions, and how-to guide
  • This proposal page is the prototype — click "View Live Demos" to explore

Questions
Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions most likely to come up in your team's review.

Can we use CMHI System of Care grant funds for this? ⌄
Yes. This platform directly supports the CMHI grant's goals of improving access to behavioral health services for children, youth, and families. The platform serves as a community-facing deliverable of the System of Care infrastructure. We can provide a scope-of-work letter formatted for grant documentation purposes, and we will submit a W-9 and complete any required vendor registration forms within 24 hours of contract signing.
What happens to the data if we stop working with OptimizED? ⌄
Your data is always yours. Under all options, you can export a complete CSV or JSON of all records at any time through the admin dashboard. Under Option B, you receive the full source code and database at project close. Under Option A, if you choose not to renew after Year 1, we will provide a full data export and source code transfer within 30 days of your notice.
How does the admin dashboard work? Can our team update records without a developer? ⌄
Yes. The admin dashboard is a secure, password-protected interface where authorized team members can add new resources, edit existing records, update contact information, and deactivate outdated listings. No coding required. We will provide a 60-minute training session and written documentation. The Region 8 team uses the same system and manages their own updates independently.
Can the platform sync with our Google Sheet automatically? ⌄
Yes, with a caveat. Your spreadsheet uses a non-standard format (shaded rows as category headers, unshaded rows as records). We built custom parsing logic for Region 8's similar structure and will replicate that for your spreadsheet, allowing your team to update the Google Sheet and sync changes to the platform. This is included in the scope at no additional cost.
Can the two regions be combined into one platform instead of two separate ones? ⌄
Yes, and this is worth discussing. A combined platform with a region selector would allow families who move between regions to see all resources in one place. The tradeoff is that each region loses its distinct branding and the ability to customize independently. We can scope a combined platform at a modest discount ($15,500 for Option A equivalent) if your team prefers that approach.
What is the procurement process? Do you need to be a registered vendor? ⌄
OptimizED Strategic Solutions is a registered business entity. We will provide a W-9, Certificate of Insurance, and complete any required ND state vendor registration within 24 hours of contract signing. We have completed this process for the Region 8 engagement and can share that documentation as a reference. A standard professional services agreement is available for your legal review.
How is this different from a simple website or a PDF directory? ⌄
A PDF or static website lists resources. This platform filters, searches, translates, and educates. A family in Fargo who speaks Spanish and needs crisis services for a 14-year-old can find exactly what they need in under 30 seconds — in their language, on their phone. A care coordinator can save a list of resources and export it as a CSV for a family meeting. That is the difference.
